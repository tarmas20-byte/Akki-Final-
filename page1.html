Impacts

The rising housing affordability has had significant social consequences on the Sydney community, resulting in a multitude of issues with residents mental health, generational inequality, and delayed life milestones.

Health Impacts

One of the most common concerns that are being discussed with APS psychologists are the rising house costs and mortgage payments. It has been stated that, “ These concerns are felt across all income levels, but they are particularly acute for low-income families and those who are already experiencing mental health problems and have less resilience.” As most people are aware, buying a house and paying it off can be a stressful experience, but as prices start rising, so do stress levels. Financial stress can lead to several psychological and physical problems such as increased heart rate, increased blood pressure, changes in appetite and sleep schedules, fatigue, irritability, and feelings and hopelessness and helplessness. When stress is prolonged so are the health effects, resulting in chronic mental health problems such as anxiety and depression, people who are affected by this are also more likely to engage in risky behaviours such as substance abuse or self-harm. The physical health effects include chronic pain, and impacts on their digestive system, heart, sleep patterns, memory, and concentration levels.

Generational Inequality

Generational inequality can be described as, ‘the unfair distribution of resources, opportunities, and economic outcomes between different age cohorts, often where younger generations bear higher burdens than older ones.’ This is a very real reality for current and especially future homeowners due to the rising housing prices. The rising house prices increase the divide between the individuals that brought their homes when they were younger and the houses were more affordable and those on modest incomes who are now shut out of home ownership and are struggling with rent and mortgage payments as they escalate. In between 2003 and 2021, homeownership amongst people aged 25 to 49 fell from 44% to 38% and amongst people aged 30-34 it fell from 57% to 50%

Delayed Life Milestones

Increased housing prices and significantly delaying key life milestones for young adults worldwide by limiting financial freedom. Due to the unaffordable purchase prices and the rising rents the median age for first-time home buyers has increased significantly over the past 25 years, rising from 24 to 33 years.
