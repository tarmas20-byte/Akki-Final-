How Rising Prices Affect Prospective Buyers

Sydney's median house price has grown over 300% in the past two decades, far outpacing wage growth. First-home buyers now need a deposit of $320,000+ just to get into the market — a figure that takes the average household over a decade to save. The result is a growing class of permanent renters and a widening gap between those who own property and those who never will.

The impact on future buyers is even starker: at current trajectory, median Sydney homes will be entirely unattainable for median earners within a decade without significant structural intervention from government at all levels.
